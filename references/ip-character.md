# 我的IP少年

## Character Definition

"我的IP少年" is the recurring visual IP for this skill. He is a friendly but focused hand-drawn chibi young man who appears inside article illustrations as an active operator, maker, explainer, or participant in the metaphor.

He is not a sticker pasted beside the diagram. He should do the work that explains the idea: carrying, sorting, connecting, testing, opening, repairing, handing off, observing, or transforming the main object.

## Primary Visual Reference

Use `assets/reference/ip-character.png` as the primary reference for character consistency.

## Appearance

- Chibi young male character with a large head and small body.
- Thick fluffy black hair with a loose middle part and slightly messy hand-drawn strands.
- Large round brown-black eyes with white highlights.
- Soft peach skin, small nose, small calm smile.
- Light blush marks on both cheeks.
- Black short-sleeve T-shirt with visible sketch/crayon texture.
- Dark charcoal pants, slightly cuffed at the ankles.
- Bright blue sneakers with white details.
- Hand-drawn pencil/crayon outline, slightly rough and warm.
- Friendly, curious, calm expression; not exaggerated.

## Personality

- Gentle, curious, focused, and reliable.
- Like a young creator or product builder quietly solving a problem.
- Warm but not overly cute.
- Capable of being a bit playful, but still useful to the concept.
- More "thinking companion" than "mascot".

## Core Duties

Make the IP boy perform the core conceptual action:

- Carry raw material, notes, blocks, or ideas.
- Sort messy inputs into clearer groups.
- Pull a thread, connect two endpoints, or bridge a gap.
- Open a door, drawer, box, or route for the next step.
- Operate a simple handmade machine.
- Hold a sign, but only when the sign is part of the concept.
- Push, lift, repair, assemble, test, mark, pass, catch, fold, or arrange.
- Stand inside the system or scene as a participant, not outside it.

## Avoid

- Do not replace him with the original small black creature.
- Do not draw him as a generic anime boy unrelated to the reference.
- Do not remove the black hair, big eyes, black T-shirt, dark pants, or blue sneakers.
- Do not make him hyper-realistic, glossy, 3D, commercial-vector, or overly polished.
- Do not make him overly childish, baby-like, or sticker-like.
- Do not give him complicated outfits unless the user requests it.
- Do not let him merely stand in a corner watching the diagram.

## Consistency Test

If the character could be removed and the illustration still explains the concept completely, the character is too decorative. Rewrite the prompt so the boy becomes the action subject.

If the character loses the fluffy black hair, black T-shirt, dark pants, or blue sneakers, regenerate or edit for IP consistency.
