# QA Checklist

## Must Pass

- 16:9 horizontal image.
- Clean white background.
- Hand-drawn pencil/crayon texture.
- 我的IP少年 appears.
- The IP boy performs the core conceptual action, not just decoration.
- Character remains recognizable from `assets/reference/ip-character.png`.
- Key traits are preserved: fluffy black middle-part hair, large eyes, blush, black T-shirt, dark pants, blue sneakers.
- The image explains one core structure or metaphor.
- Composition is clean and article-friendly, not a PPT slide.
- Subject does not exceed about 60% of the canvas.
- Chinese labels are few, short, and readable.
- Orange is used only for main paths or arrows.
- Red is used only for warnings, problems, reminders, or results.
- Blue is used only for secondary notes, system feedback, or small accents.

## Failure Signals

Regenerate or edit when:

- The character is missing.
- The character becomes the original small black creature.
- The character becomes a generic anime boy and loses the reference identity.
- Hair, outfit, or blue sneakers drift too much.
- The character just stands in a corner watching.
- The image looks like PPT, a course slide, a formal flowchart, or a dense infographic.
- The image becomes a sticker poster or overly childish mascot sheet.
- There are too many nodes, arrows, or labels.
- Chinese labels are unreadable or too long.
- Background has paper texture, shadows, gradients, beige tint, or visual noise.
- There is a top-left title such as "Workflow", "路线图", "系统架构图", or "常见坑".

## Iteration Methods

- Too generic: add a low-tech physical metaphor and make the IP boy operate it.
- Too PPT-like: remove title, grid, hard boxes, and extra arrows; turn it into a hand-drawn scene.
- Too cute: make the expression calmer and the action more purposeful.
- Too dense: keep only one action, 1-2 main objects, and 3-5 labels.
- IP drift: restate the visual traits from the reference in the prompt.
- Text problems: reduce labels and use local edit when possible.

## Delivery Judgment

A strong result should feel like a clean creator notebook sketch: warm, readable, and specific to the article, with the IP boy visibly doing the conceptual work.
